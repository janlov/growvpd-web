Tuya Cloud Bridge: Deep Dive

If you’ve already followed our 5-step Cloud Bridge wizard and want to understand why it works the way it does — what’s in your phone, what we never see, and where it’s all going — this is the page for you. The wizard is the “how”; this guide is the full “what and why”.

1. The Cloud Bridge concept

Tuya is a B2B IoT platform. Brands like BlitzWolf, Treatlife, Moes, Lonsonho, Qingping, Aqara (some lines), Sonoff (some lines) license Tuya’s firmware and cloud. When you buy a “smart plug” from Amazon for $9, there is a 70% chance it’s really a Tuya plug under a brand sticker.

For mains-powered devices, we can talk to them directly over your Wi-Fi (see the LAN guide). But battery sensors, cameras, and certain “cloud-only” categories never accept inbound connections; they only push data to Tuya’s cloud. To read that data, we need an authenticated session against the Tuya cloud, scoped to your devices.

The Cloud Bridge is exactly that: a per-user authenticated channel. You create a free Tuya developer trial, get a unique access_id + access_secret, link your Smart Life devices to it, and paste the credentials into our wizard. From then on, GrowVPD on your phone polls Tuya’s OpenAPI directly — not via our servers.

Why this isn’t shady. The credentials live only on your phone, encrypted with the Android Keystore. Our backend literally never sees them. You can verify this in SecureCredentialStore.kt.

2. Why no one-tap OAuth (yet)

The natural question: “Why isn’t this just a Login-with-Tuya button like Sign-in-with-Google?”

The honest answer: Tuya OEM partnerships start at roughly $25,000/year for the “Smart Home PaaS OEM” tier that gives third-party apps an OAuth client ID. We are a 1–2 person team building this in public. We can’t justify that spend until we hit ~1,000 paid users (that’s our internal break-even).

So the trial-account route is a practical workaround. It takes 15–30 minutes once, then forgets about it forever. Our roadmap commitment: once we hit 1,000 paid users, we apply for the OEM tier and the OAuth one-tap replaces the wizard automatically. Existing wizard users keep their setup; new users see a button instead.

Until then, here’s the full setup with the wizard.

3. Step-by-step with screenshots

Step 1

Open the wizard in GrowVPD

From the Automation tab: Add platform → Tuya → “I have cloud-only sensors”. The wizard greets you with an explanation of what you’re about to do.

Screenshot reference: tuya-bridge-step1.png — the wizard intro screen with green “Start” button. Available in app at R.drawable.guide_tuya_bridge_step1.
Step 2

Create a free Tuya IoT trial account

The wizard opens an in-app browser pointed at iot.tuya.com/cloud/. You sign up with your email (use any), verify, and the Tuya console appears. Tap + Create Cloud Project:

  • Project name: e.g. GrowVPD — only you see this.
  • Description: optional.
  • Industry: “Smart Home”.
  • Development method: “Smart Home”.
  • Data center: pick the region nearest your devices — see section 4.

You get a 6-month trial. After the trial, click “Renew” in the console and it extends, free, indefinitely as long as you keep using the project.

Screenshot reference: tuya-iot-create-project.png — the Tuya console “Create Cloud Project” dialog.
Step 3

Subscribe to the required APIs

Inside your Cloud Project, go to Service API → Go to authorize. Subscribe to all three APIs the wizard lists:

  1. IoT Core — the foundation API; lets us read device status and send commands.
  2. Smart Home Basic Service — gives us the Smart Life device list and DP definitions.
  3. Smart Home Pulsar Service — the websocket push channel that streams sensor changes in real time.

All three are on the free trial. Tap “I subscribed” in the wizard when done.

Step 4

Link your Smart Life account

In the project: Devices → Link Tuya App Account → Add App Account. A QR code appears. Scan it with your Smart Life app: Me → Settings → (top-right scan icon) → scan. Smart Life pops a confirmation; tap Authorize.

This step tells Tuya’s cloud “expose this user’s devices to this project’s API token”. It’s the consent step, equivalent to an OAuth Authorize click.

Screenshot reference: tuya-link-smart-life.png — the QR scan and Smart Life authorize popup.
Step 5

Paste credentials and validate

Back in the wizard, paste from the project Overview tab:

  • Access ID / Client ID — long alphanumeric string, ~20 chars
  • Access Secret / Client Secret — longer string, ~32 chars
  • Region — auto-filled from the data centre you picked in step 2

Tap Validate. The wizard:

  1. Computes an HMAC-SHA256 signature over (client_id || timestamp || nonce || string_to_sign) — this is Tuya’s authentication scheme.
  2. Posts it to https://openapi.tuyaeu.com/v1.0/token?grant_type=1.
  3. Receives an access_token + refresh_token + 7200-second expiry.
  4. Calls /v1.0/iot-01/associated-users/{uid}/devices to fetch your device list.
  5. Stores everything encrypted in SecureCredentialStore and shows the device picker.

Pick the devices you want to import, assign them to tents, and tap Add. You’re done.

4. Region selection: when to pick which

Tuya has five data centres. Your Smart Life account is bound to exactly one. If you guess wrong, validation will fail with a misleading error. Match it to where you originally signed up for Smart Life.

EU (Central)openapi.tuyaeu.comYou’re in Europe and your phone signed up there. Default for CZ/DE/PL/etc.

Not sure which region you’re on? Open Smart Life: Me → Settings → Account and Security. The “Region” field tells you. If you accidentally created a Tuya cloud project in the wrong region, just delete it and create a new one — takes 30 seconds.

5. API quota and how we batch calls

The Tuya free trial has soft and hard limits. Soft limit: 50 devices visible per project. Hard limit: 1 million API calls per month. We’ve never seen a user hit the hard limit (that would be ~23 calls per minute, every minute, for a month) but the device cap matters.

If you’re at 50+ Tuya devices — rare but possible if you’re multi-room, multi-tent — here’s how to handle it:

  • Most plugs/lights/fans should be on LAN, not Cloud Bridge. Move them to LAN to free Cloud Bridge slots.
  • Cloud Bridge is for sensors and cameras only. If you have 50 sensors, congratulations, you’re a commercial grow — reach out and we’ll help arrange the paid Tuya tier.

How we batch to stay polite

Our polling cadence is intentionally conservative:

  • Sensor poll: every 5 minutes (default), configurable to 10/15 in Settings.
  • Device-status poll: every 10 minutes, batched so each tent goes out as a single call.
  • Pulsar push: always-on websocket, doesn’t count against poll quota.

Realistic monthly usage for a typical 8-device setup: ~85,000 calls/month. That’s 8.5% of the trial cap.

6. Token refresh and auto-recovery

Tuya access tokens expire after 7,200 seconds (2 hours). Refresh tokens last 30 days. GrowVPD handles both transparently:

  1. Background refresh ~10 minutes before expiry. If it succeeds, you never know it happened.
  2. If background refresh fails (rare; usually network blip), the next API call detects the 401 and triggers an immediate refresh attempt.
  3. If the refresh token has expired (only happens if app was offline > 30 days), GrowVPD pops a one-tap notification: “Tuya credentials need re-validation, tap to reopen wizard”. Re-validation takes ~10 seconds and your device list is preserved.
# Token refresh sequence (simplified)
GET /v1.0/token/<refresh_token> with HMAC headers
→ { access_token, refresh_token, expire_time: 7200 }
→ persist to SecureCredentialStore (Android Keystore-backed)
→ schedule next refresh in 6,800 s

7. Pulsar streaming explained

Polling every 5 minutes is fine for slow-changing sensors but bad for events — you don’t want to wait 5 minutes to know a temp probe just spiked. That’s where Tuya’s Pulsar service comes in.

Pulsar is a websocket-based push channel. Once you subscribe (the wizard does this in step 3), Tuya’s cloud opens an MQTT-over-WSS connection from your phone and any device state change pushes a message in real time (typically < 1 second from device wake to phone).

This is critical for battery TH sensors: they wake every ~10 minutes, post a reading, and sleep. Pulsar makes sure that reading lands in your diary the moment it arrives at Tuya’s cloud, instead of waiting for the next poll cycle to discover it.

What you’ll see in the app. Sensor tiles update with a small lightning bolt icon when they came in via Pulsar push, vs a clock icon for a polled reading. This helps you confirm Pulsar is working.

8. Troubleshooting

“1106 invalid signature”

The HMAC computed by GrowVPD didn’t match what Tuya expected. 95% of the time the cause is a stray space or newline in the pasted Access ID or Secret. Re-copy from the Tuya console using the “Copy” icon next to each field, paste again, validate.

5% of the time the cause is a system clock skew. The HMAC includes a timestamp; if your phone’s clock is off by more than ~5 minutes from Tuya’s NTP, signatures fail. Open phone Settings → Date & Time → Set automatically. Toggle off and back on to force resync.

“1004 region mismatch”

You created the Tuya project in (say) US, but your Smart Life account is EU. The token validates but the device list call fails because your devices live on a different shard. Fix:

  1. In Tuya console, delete the wrong-region project.
  2. Re-create in the correct region (see section 4).
  3. Re-paste the new Access ID + Secret in the wizard.

“Token expired”

If you see this and auto-refresh didn’t kick in, two possibilities:

  • App was offline for > 30 days — refresh token expired. Re-run the wizard’s Validate step (no need to redo steps 1-4); takes 10 seconds.
  • Tuya project was deleted in the console. Look at your Tuya console; if the project is gone, re-create.

“No devices found after validate”

You skipped or fumbled step 4 (linking Smart Life). In Tuya console, go back to Devices → Link Tuya App Account and confirm your Smart Life username appears in the linked accounts list. If not, re-do the QR + Authorize.

“Some devices show, others don’t”

The Tuya OpenAPI only exposes devices that were assigned to a “Home” in Smart Life. Devices in “Default” sometimes don’t enumerate. Open Smart Life, create a Home if you don’t have one, drag the missing devices into it. Wait 30 seconds, retry the GrowVPD validate.

“Pulsar push isn’t working”

You’ll know from the lightning-bolt vs clock icon (see section 7). Causes:

  • Smart Home Pulsar Service subscription was missed in step 3. Go back to Tuya console, subscribe.
  • Your phone’s battery saver killed the websocket. Enable Hub Mode (recommended) or whitelist GrowVPD from battery optimisation.

9. Privacy: where credentials live

Plain English: your Tuya credentials never touch our servers. They live in three places, all on your phone:

  • SecureCredentialStore — an EncryptedSharedPreferences instance backed by the Android Keystore. The encryption key is held in the Tee/StrongBox secure enclave; even root access on your phone can’t extract it directly.
  • The Tuya OpenAPI access tokens, refreshed every 2 hours.
  • An in-memory cache of decoded device list, refreshed each app start.

What we, the GrowVPD team, can see: nothing related to Tuya. We have no logs of your access ID, no copy of your token, no idea which devices you have. Crashlytics opt-in collects stack traces only and we explicitly redact Tuya keys.

What Tuya can see: the same as if you used Smart Life. Your device states, sensor readings, on/off events. Nothing we add to the picture.

10. Future: when we get OEM approval, OAuth replaces this wizard

Once we cross the Tuya OEM threshold (~1,000 paid users; current state: applying after launch ramp), the user-facing flow becomes:

  1. Tap “Connect Tuya” in GrowVPD.
  2. Custom Tab opens to auth.tuyaeu.com/login with our app’s OAuth client ID.
  3. Sign in with Smart Life credentials, tap Authorize.
  4. Custom Tab returns to the app with an OAuth code.
  5. App exchanges code for tokens, picks devices, done.

Total time: ~30 seconds vs today’s 15–30 minutes. The wizard’s job ends. We are eager to ship this and are tracking the user-count milestone publicly on Discord.

Until then: the manual wizard is fully functional, fully private, and once-and-done. Many of our most loyal beta users have it set up and never thought about it again.

11. FAQ

Q: How long does the Tuya free trial actually last?

6 months at first. After that, click “Renew Trial” in the Tuya console; it extends free for another 6 months. Repeat indefinitely. We’ve had users on the trial path for 18+ months without paying Tuya a cent.

Q: Can I share my Tuya credentials with my partner so we both use the same project?

Technically yes — you can paste the same Access ID/Secret on two phones. But for clean accountability we recommend each person creates their own Tuya project (free) and links the same Smart Life account separately. That way revoking one doesn’t affect the other.

Q: What if Tuya changes their pricing or shuts down trials?

We watch this carefully. If trials disappear, we’ll write a migration guide to whatever the new entry-level path is, or accelerate our OEM application. Either way, the data your phone has cached keeps working — you’re never stranded.

Q: Why does GrowVPD ask for my Smart Life login during LAN setup?

That’s a one-shot, not Cloud Bridge. LAN needs the per-device localKey to encrypt commands. The fastest way to fetch keys is via Smart Life’s lightweight API. Cloud Bridge is a different path that fetches keys + provides cloud streaming — you only need it for battery sensors.

Q: What if my Tuya project gets suspended?

This is rare but happens if you exceed the free trial’s API quota or violate Tuya’s ToS (e.g. trying to resell their data). The fix is to create a new project — keep the same Smart Life account — and re-paste the new credentials. Your GrowVPD history (diary, sensor readings) is unaffected.

Q: Can I export my Tuya project data if I switch to another grow app later?

Your sensor history is stored in your local GrowVPD database (Room), and Pro users can export it as JSON anytime. The Tuya project itself is yours — you control it via the Tuya console regardless of which app you use.

Q: Does Cloud Bridge work without internet?

No. The whole point of Cloud Bridge is to reach devices that only speak to the Tuya cloud. If your internet is down, those devices can’t reach the cloud anyway. Mains-powered devices on LAN keep working fine during internet outages.

Q: Do my Tuya credentials sync across multiple phones if I use Multi-Device Sync?

No, deliberately. Each phone holds its own Tuya credentials. This is a security feature: revoking access on one phone doesn’t leak to another. See Multi-Device Sync Setup for what does and doesn’t sync.
