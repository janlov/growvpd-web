Three pairing paths for Tuya. A spare phone becomes a 24/7 hub. Two phones share one diary — over local Wi-Fi, no internet required.

Three pairing paths for Tuya

Path A

LAN direct

For 230 V mains devices: smart plugs, AC units, lights, fans, valves. v3.5 GCM protocol. Sub-second response. Zero cloud calls.

Path B

Cloud Bridge wizard

For battery WiFi sensors (wsdcg, co2bj) and foreign-region devices. 5-step guided wizard pairs your own Tuya IoT trial with the app. Your credentials, encrypted on device.

Path C

Zigbee via hub

For Aqara, Sonoff, Tuya Zigbee sensors. Live data + multi-year battery life. Pair through Blow Smart Hub or Sonoff ZBDongle. Recommended for serious sensor coverage.

Rule of thumb: mains plug? Path A. Battery sensor? Path C if you can, Path B if you must. The app tells you which path fits your device the moment you add it.

Hub Mode — turn an old phone into your grow guardian

Got an old Android in a drawer? Plug it in next to the tent, install GrowVPD, enable Hub Mode. It becomes a 24/7 sync anchor and automation runner. Foreground service survives reboots, battery whitelist keeps it awake, persistent notification keeps Android happy. ~5 minutes of setup, runs forever on a 4–5-year-old phone.

Different from a Tuya Zigbee hub: that’s for sub-devices. Hub Mode is for keeping your app running 24/7 so automation rules fire on time, sensor data logs continuously, and your other phones stay in sync even when they’re asleep.

~5 min setup time

Multi-device sync — two phones, same diary

Pair phones with a one-time QR code. After that, your diary, photos and sensor history sync over local Wi-Fi automatically. Encrypted with Ed25519 keys derived at pairing — the data never crosses our hands, never touches a central server, doesn’t need internet at all.

Off-LAN fallback: if you’re on cellular while your partner’s home, an optional WebSocket relay (Bring Your Own Host) carries the encrypted blob blindly. You can self-host it on a Raspberry Pi or Oracle Cloud Always Free tier today; an official relay is on the 6-month roadmap.

FAQ

Honest answers to real questions.

If yours isn’t here, jump on Discord — you’ll get a reply, not a ticket number.

Does it really work without internet?
Yes — the app is local-first. VPD chart, diary, calculators, equipment DB and guides work fully offline. Smart automation needs internet only because your gear’s cloud requires it (Tuya Cloud, Mars Hydro MQTT, etc.). LAN-direct Tuya doesn’t need internet at all.
How are you different from Grow with Jane or Photone?
Grow with Jane and Photone are excellent diary apps. We are a diary app plus automation across 8 brands — the only one that lets a Tuya plug react to an AC Infinity sensor reading, all from one rule. Plus 11 languages, plus offline-first, plus no central server. Different category.
What about HomeAssistant? Why not just use that?
HA is fantastic if you already run it. We’re for growers who don’t want to host a Pi, configure YAML, or learn ESPHome to monitor humidity. A HomeAssistant bridge is on the 6-month roadmap so you can run both side by side.
Why is launch pricing so low?
Because we need users more than we need cash today. $3.99/mo or $49.99 lifetime is intentionally aggressive — once we have a track record and a few thousand happy growers, prices will rise to a sustainable level. Locking in lifetime now means you keep what you paid for, forever.
What if Tuya bans your trial account?
Honest answer: our shared Tuya trial caps at 50 devices — we never relied on it for scale. The Cloud Bridge wizard pairs your own Tuya IoT trial (free, takes 15 min). At ~1,000 paid users we can afford the commercial tier ($25k/yr). Path A (LAN direct) and Path C (Zigbee) don’t need any Tuya cloud at all.
Can I migrate my old grow logs?
Diary import from Grow with Jane & Photone is on the roadmap (next 6 months). Until then, you can keep your old app running while you set up here in parallel — nothing breaks, no rush.
Lifetime — what if the company shuts down?
Your data lives on your phone, not our server — even if VPD Labs LLC vanished tomorrow, your diary, photos and settings stay with you, exportable to PDF/JSON. The app keeps working until Android/iOS itself stops supporting the SDK level. Realistically that’s years.
How do I get beta access?
Join Discord and ask in #beta. Android: APK link or Play Store internal-test invite. iOS: TestFlight invite (limited slots while we build the iOS app). We onboard new testers in waves so we can keep up with feedback.
I have a Raspberry Pi — can I use it as a hub?
Not yet — today Hub Mode is Android-only (use any spare 4–5-year-old phone). Pi/JVM hub agent is on the next-6-months roadmap. We’ll publish the reference Ktor implementation so you can self-host before we ship the official binary.
Will pricing rise later?
Yes — once we’ve proven the app at 1,000+ paid users, monthly will likely move to $5–9, lifetime to $99–149. Existing buyers always keep what they paid for. If you grab Lifetime now at $49.99, that’s your price for life.
